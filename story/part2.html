Part 2: Goober world Levels 3-12

'out of battery' Level 3

Chargy:  I've run out of charge...

???:  Really? Just, go to the battery and refill. How many times have you done this?

???:  Zero?

Only real purpose of this was to show the player that chargy has a limited amount of charge; You need to get charge from a battery before you can charge a phone.
Level 4 has the first major use of Symbolism. This game, as it progresses, will use symbolism more and more. In this case, if you see a block with a bunch of 't's on it, you can pass right though it.
Level 6 introduces the Bounce mechanic. If you hold the down key you accelerate downwards, if you hit something you bounce right back up. If you release down right after you bounce, you can get a lot of height. I love this movement option, however it is a little tricky to understand at first. Note that you cannot bounce after a jump, and you can only bounce once before fully landing, unless you have charge.
At level 7, you are introduced to the concept of multiple phones. You have to charge both of them to teleport to the next level.

'Goobers?' Level 8

Chargy:  What where those things?

???:  Those are Goobers. More specifically, Grass Goobers. They are the main inhabitants of this world.

???:  They are very friendly, but they can be a bit... aggressive when provoked.

Goobers and many other sprites were created by my brother, Trey. I did have to adjust the art a little to either   f it the game's theme more or have a bit more fluid/fitting animation, but the original art and concept was all him.
At level 10, you have reached the surface! You can see the sky, and in front of you is a unfamiliar ememy. A brunk. If you get too close to it, it will lift up its arm and attack you. However, if you back up in time, it's arm will be a platform you can jump off off. This level also introduces the first Secret phone. These are optional phones that are not required to progress initially, and are always visible, but are inaccessable in either knowledge, skill, or a physical barrier.
At level 11, you are taught how to kill a brunk. It's the first time where charge is used outside of charging a phone.
Level 12. The final level of Goober world. It's also the first Progressive level - a level that has multiple connected sections. Beating this will trigger the first major cutscene of the game, where you go to the spacestation to meet the mysterious '???' character that has been talking to you since the start of the game.
